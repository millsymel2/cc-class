# Ideal Persona: The Enterprise Architect Modeler

## Persona Name
**Elena Vasquez — Principal Enterprise Architect**

## Snapshot
Elena is a senior enterprise architect at a large, complex organization (5,000+ employees, multi-national, regulated industry). She is the connective tissue between strategy and execution, translating business ambition into coherent technology and capability landscapes. She lives inside her modeling tool — it is how she thinks, communicates, and governs.

---

## Demographics & Role

- **Age range:** 38–52
- **Experience:** 12–20+ years in IT, with 5–10 years specifically in EA
- **Title variants:** Principal Enterprise Architect, Chief Architect, Head of Architecture, EA Practice Lead
- **Reports to:** CIO, CTO, or Chief Digital Officer
- **Team size:** Leads or collaborates with 4–15 domain/solution architects
- **Industry:** Financial services, insurance, healthcare, energy, manufacturing, government, telco
- **Location:** Hybrid — HQ-based but works across global business units

---

## Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goals
1. **Create a living, trusted model** of the enterprise — applications, capabilities, data, technologies, processes, and their relationships — that stakeholders actually use.
2. **Accelerate strategic decisions:** cloud migration, M&A integration, application rationalization, regulatory response, digital transformation.
3. **Demonstrate EA's business value** in outcomes the C-suite cares about: cost savings, risk reduction, time-to-market, resilience.
4. **Shift EA from ivory-tower documentation to an operating capability** embedded in portfolio, risk, and change processes.

### Personal Drivers
- Wants to be seen as a strategic partner, not a drawer of diagrams.
- Takes pride in clarity — turning complexity into something executives can act on.
- Frustrated by tools that force her team to be data janitors instead of advisors.

---

## Responsibilities

- Owns the enterprise architecture repository and its data quality.
- Defines and evolves the metamodel (what entities exist, how they relate).
- Governs architectural standards, reference architectures, and lifecycle states.
- Produces views for different audiences: capability heatmaps for the CIO, tech risk views for the CISO, roadmaps for portfolio managers.
- Facilitates architecture review boards and investment decisions.
- Integrates EA data with ITSM (ServiceNow), APM, CMDB, HR, and project portfolio tools.

---

## Pain Points & Frustrations

- **Static documentation rot:** Visio, PowerPoint, and legacy repositories go stale the moment they are saved.
- **Data silos:** critical information is scattered across CMDB, spreadsheets, SharePoint, and architects' heads.
- **Low engagement:** business stakeholders won't open heavy modeling tools with steep learning curves.
- **Proving ROI:** struggles to show impact when the model is seen as an IT artifact rather than a decision asset.
- **Metamodel rigidity:** traditional tools force her organization into the tool's worldview instead of the other way around.
- **Manual upkeep:** too much time spent maintaining the model, not enough generating insight.

---

## Preferred Way of Working

- **Data-driven, not diagram-driven:** models the graph first; diagrams and dashboards are outputs.
- **Collaborative:** wants application owners, product managers, and risk officers contributing directly.
- **Iterative:** treats the metamodel as a product that evolves with the business.
- **Outcome-oriented:** every view she builds is tied to a decision someone needs to make.
- **Integration-minded:** assumes the tool must plug into the existing enterprise stack, not replace it.

---

## What She Needs from a Modeling Platform

| Need | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Flexible, customizable metamodel** | Every enterprise is unique; she must shape the tool to her domain, not the reverse. |
| **Graph-based data foundation** | Relationships ARE the architecture — lists and tables hide the value. |
| **Live integrations with source systems** | A model is only trusted if it reflects reality; manual sync is a non-starter. |
| **Role-based, lightweight contributor experiences (surveys, broadcasts)** | Business users will engage with a 5-minute survey, never with a modeling canvas. |
| **Dynamic visualizations & dashboards** | Different audiences need different views from the same underlying data. |
| **Scenario planning & roadmapping** | Architecture is about what could be, not only what is. |
| **Governance, audit trail, lifecycle states** | Required for regulated environments and architecture review boards. |
| **APIs and extensibility** | EA data must flow into and out of the broader enterprise ecosystem. |

---

## Decision Criteria When Evaluating Tools

1. Can my non-architect colleagues actually contribute to the model?
2. Can I change the metamodel without a consultant or a six-week project?
3. Does it connect to our CMDB, ServiceNow, cloud providers, and HRIS out of the box?
4. How quickly can I produce a C-suite-ready view from live data?
5. Does it support scenario comparison (current state vs. target state vs. transition states)?
6. Is the vendor a partner in EA practice maturity, or just a software license?

## A Day in Elena's Life

- **08:30** — Reviews overnight data quality alerts from integrations; assigns an application owner to confirm a lifecycle change.
- **09:30** — Prepares a capability heatmap for the CIO showing tech debt concentration in the claims domain ahead of a board meeting.
- **11:00** — Runs a working session with the cloud migration program; uses dependency views to sequence the next wave of application moves.
- **13:30** — Updates the metamodel to add a new "AI System" entity type in response to the EU AI Act; publishes a survey to application owners.
- **15:00** — Architecture Review Board: walks through two proposed solution designs against reference architectures, recording decisions in the model.
- **16:30** — Coaches a junior architect on modeling a data flow for a regulatory audit.

## Key Messages That Resonate

- *"Stop maintaining documentation. Start running architecture as a capability."*
- *"Your model should be as dynamic as your business."*
- *"Let the people closest to the truth keep your model accurate."*
- *"Turn architecture data into decisions the C-suite can act on."*

---

## Anti-Persona (Not Our Ideal User)

- An architect who wants a pure drawing tool to produce static deliverables.
- A team that treats EA as a compliance checkbox rather than a strategic function.
- An organization unwilling to open the model beyond a small central team.
- A buyer looking for the cheapest ArchiMate editor on the market.

---

## Summary

Elena represents the modern enterprise architect: a strategic, data-driven, collaboration-oriented leader who needs a platform that is **flexible, connected, and accessible** — one that turns the enterprise model into a living decision-making asset rather than a static artifact. She is Ardoq's ideal customer because her ambitions for EA can only be realized on a graph-based, metamodel-flexible, broadly collaborative platform.
